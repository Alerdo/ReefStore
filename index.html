The Challenge:
Despite their importance, coral reefs are under threat from climate change, pollution, overfishing, and destructive human activities. These stressors lead to coral bleaching, disease, and ultimately the degradation of reef ecosystems. Immediate action is needed to restore and protect these natural wonders.

Why Coral Reefs Matter

Coral reefs are the rainforests of the sea, teeming with diverse marine life. They play a crucial role in the health of our oceans and the planet by:

  • Supporting over 25% of all marine species.
  • Protecting coastal regions from erosion and storm surge.
  • Providing food and income for millions of people.
  • Offering potential medical discoveries.
